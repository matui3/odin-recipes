Hong Kong Style Egg Tart

Description

Hong Kong-style egg tarts are a beloved pastry featuring a flaky, buttery crust filled with a silky-smooth egg custard. These bite-sized delights strike the perfect balance between rich and light, with a crisp, layered exterior that shatters delicately to reveal a jiggly, slightly sweetened center. Inspired by Portuguese pastéis de nata but refined in Cantonese bakeries, they're a staple of dim sum culture—best enjoyed fresh from the oven with a cup of milky tea.

What sets them apart is their texture: the crust, made with either puff pastry or a shortcrust dough, contrasts beautifully with the creamy, caramelized custard. Simple ingredients like eggs, sugar, and evaporated milk create a fragrant, velvety filling that's barely set—like a cross between crème brûlée and flan. Whether eaten warm or at room temperature, these golden tarts embody Hong Kong's fusion of East-meets-West culinary charm.

Ingredients

  • For the crust:
    • 200g all-purpose flour
    • 115g cold unsalted butter, cubed
    • 2 tbsp powdered sugar
    • 1 egg yolk
    • 1-2 tbsp ice water (if needed)
  • For the custard filling:
    • 3 large eggs
    • 100g granulated sugar
    • 200ml hot water
    • 100ml evaporated milk
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Make the crust dough:
    1. Mix flour, powdered sugar, and cold butter until crumbly.
    2. Add egg yolk and knead into a smooth dough (add ice water if needed).
    3. Wrap in plastic and chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the custard:
    1. Dissolve sugar in hot water, then cool to room temperature.
    2. Whisk in eggs, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract.
    3. Strain the mixture through a fine sieve for smoothness.
  3. Assemble:
    1. Roll out the dough to 3mm thickness and cut circles to line tart molds.
    2. Pour custard into shells (fill 80% to prevent overflow).
  4. Bake:
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Bake for 15-20 minutes until crust is golden and custard jiggles slightly.
    3. Cool in molds for 5 minutes, then transfer to a rack.
